COGNITION AND MENTAL HEALTH


-Gayathri

By: Hues Of The Mind

Posted on January 1, 2019 at 12:00 PM

Mental health is not only the general state of mind, but also encompasses the way surroundings are perceived, the understanding about an occurrence and the perspective chosen to take on said occurrences.

What is cognition?

There are a set of cognitive skills that usually help us take decisions:

  • Sustained attention: is where we look at, listen and think about the occurrence or situation over a certain amount of time
  • Selective attention: helps us to stay focused on a task despite possible distractions.
  • Divided attention: helps pay attention to more than one task at the same time
  • Long-term memory: is where information from the past is stored, and can be recollected from, in the future
  • Working memory: also called short term memory, helps us to retain the information while we are still processing and using it.
  • Logic and reasoning: comes in when we tend to reason, form ideas, or trying to solve problems.
  • Auditory processing: allows us to analyse and blend various segments of sounds that our brain creates.
  • Visual processing: gives us a visual aid to process information better.
  • Processing speed: allows us to perform and execute tasks quickly and accurately.

When all these cognitive skills work hand in hand, our mental health functions better. But when they tend to lag and not perform adequately, our mental health takes a toll.
