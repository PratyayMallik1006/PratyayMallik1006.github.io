PANIC DISORDER

By: Hues Of The Mind

Posted on January 1, 2019 at 12:00 PM

Panic disorder is a type of an anxiety disorder. It is characterised by recurrent occurrences of panic attacks. Panic attacks can occur at any time, any place and sometimes with no particular or specific cause for the attack. Symptoms of panic attack include heart racing, palpitations, sweating, shaking, trembling, chest pain, trembling, shortness of breath and so on. It has not yet been normalised as a mental health issue. People still generalise it with normal fear and anxiety. But that is not the case.

Excessive caffeine, alcohol can increase anxiety and panic attacks. The attack can be short lived or last until help is provided. In order to prevent panic attacks, the affected individuals may avoid certain situations. This can lead to the development of phobias. Panic attacks can even occur during sleep. These are called as nocturnal panic attacks.

The experience of a panic attack is different for different people. Panic attacks cannot be controlled. They can be triggered by certain events or stimuli. Sometimes, the affected individuals move away from the public because of a sense of shame. Education, therapy and most importantly, a person needs to be patient, sensitive and understanding to offer the best help to the affected individual
